Depict attributes pertaining to flight departure delays¶

by Benjamin UWIMANA MAHUKU¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at reasons of delays for flights, how they occur in time and how they connect to unique carriers and airports from which flights originated. The main focus was on following features of a flight: year, month, day of month, day of week, departure delay, carrier delay, weather delay, NAS delay, security delay, late aircraft delay, unique carrier, and origin airport.

Dataset Overview¶

This dataset reports flights in the United States, including carriers, arrival and departure delays, and reasons for delays, from 1987 to 2008. The data comes originally from RITA as the website says.
Given the size of dataset for each year, only three of them (1988, 1998 and 2008) were combined to form single file for this analysis. There are 17,596,545 flights in the clean dataset with 15 features.

Distribution of departure delays¶

Departure delays has a wide range of values expressed in number of minutes. It contains some unusually long period of departure delays that go beyond 1500 minutes that are more than 24 hours of delay. I considered those kind of delays as outliers or just canceled flights. Removing extreme values and plotting it gives a right skewed shape portraying how departure delays are diverse.

Categories of departure delays¶

As departure delays are really diverse, we put them in different categories of delays based on their length as follows:

  • less than 30 minutes ==> short
  • between 30 and 60 minutes ==> Acceptable
  • between 60 and 120 minutes ==> Moderately Long
  • between 120 and 180 minutes ==> Long
  • between 180 and 240 minutes ==> Very Long
  • more than 240 minutes ==> Unusually Very Long.

After assigning the above categories to all departure delays, we see that most of them (almost 70% of all departure delays) are under short and acceptable categories, while very long and unusually very long categories, taken together, have 3% of all departure delays.

Delay reasons vs Departure delay¶

Plotting flight departure delays and delay reasons (departure delay, carrier delay, weather delay, NAS delay, security delay, and late aircraft delay) shows that only carrier and late aircraft delay reasons have moderate positive relationship with actual departure delays.

But when we isolate very long and unusually very long departure delays, we observe that weather and carrier are most correlated delay reasons with actual departure delays (see plots of this in the longer version of exploration of this dataset).

Magnitude of departure delay reasons¶

Of all reasons, we notice that Weather Delay is causing longest departure delays (around 80 minutes on average per month). On the other hand, Security Delay is having shortest departure delays (around 35 minutes on average per month). The same trend is observed when we look at average departure delays per day of week.

Impact of departure delay reasons on origine airports¶

Again for visibility sake, we'll look at first 30 longest departure delays. Same trend of relationship between unique carriers and reasons of departure delays is observed also between origin airports and departure delay reasons. Still weather and carrier delay reasons are affecting most airports than other reasons of departure delays.

Though one could expect Security impacting negatively performance of flights in terms of departure delays because of security checks on different airports, data I have proves that it is not the case. Maybe because other security measures put in place like long time to report to airport before departure, which is a burden on shoulders of passengers instead, but good for everyones safety.
